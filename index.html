Passage 1

The Maratha were subdued in a series of wars. In the first war that ended in 1782 with the treaty of Slabai, there was no clear victory. The Second Anglo-Maratha War (1803-1805) was fought on different fronts, resulting in the British gaining Orrisa and the territories north of the Yamuna River including Agra and Delhi. Finnally, the third Anglo-Maratha War of 1817-1819 crushed Maratha's power. The Peshwa was removed and sent away to Bithur near Kanpur with a pension. THe company now had complete over the territories south of the Vindhyas.

Passage 2

The final wave of annexation occurred under Lord Dalhousie, who was the Governer-General from 1848. He devise da policy that came to be known as the Doctrine of Lapse. The doctrine that if an Indian ruler died without a male heir, his kingdom would "Laspe", that is, become part of company territory. One kingdom after another was annexed simply by applying this doctrine: Satara (1848), Sambalpur (1850), Udaipur (1852), Nagpur (1853) and Jhansi (1854).
